After diving deep into how technology transforms each financial statement, here's your essential scorecard. These are the metrics that separate genuine digital transformation from expensive technology theatre.

**1 - THE BIG THREE RATIOS**

**1.1 Cost-to-Income Ratio (Income Statement)** *The ultimate efficiency test*

- **What it Measures:** Operating expenses ÷ Operating Income
- **Technology Impact:** Automation and digital channels should drive this down consistently
- **Benchmark:** Best-in-Class banks &lt;50%, Retailers &lt;85%
- **Red Flag:** Ratio Increasing Despite Technology Investment

**1.2 Asset Turnover (Balance Sheet)** *How hard your assets work*

- **What it Measures:** Revenue ÷ Average Total Assets
- **Technology Impact:** Digital platforms generate more revenue per pound of assets
- **Watch for:** Increasing ratio as technology enables asset-light growth
- **Example:** TymeBank's digital model achieves 10× higher asset turnover than traditional banks

**1.3 Free Cash Flow Margin (Cash Flow)** *The truth detector*

- **What it Measures:** (Operating Cash Flow - CAPEX) ÷ Revenue
- **Technology Impact:** Should improve as automation reduces costs and capex needs
- **Key Insight:** Technology investments must ultimately improve this metric
- **Timeline:** Expect 2-3 years for technology investments to show positive impact

**2 - CROSS-STATEMENT POWER METRICS**

**2.1 Return on Technology Investment**

- **Formula:** (Annual Cash Flow Improvement + Cost Savings) ÷ Technology Investment
- **Target:** >25% for sustainable competitive advantage
- **Track quarterly:** Technology ROI should compound over time

**2.2 Digital Revenue Growth Rate**

- **Formula:** (Digital Revenue year 2 - Digital Revenue year 1) ÷ Digital Revenue year 1
- **Benchmark:** Should exceed traditional channel growth by 2-3×
- **Quality check:** Ensure digital revenue has better margins than traditional channels

**2.3 Working Capital Efficiency**

- **Formula:** Revenue ÷ (Current Assets - Current Liabilities)
- **Technology impact:** Automation should dramatically improve this ratio
- **Cash benefit:** Each day reduced in cash conversion cycle = Revenue ÷ 365 in freed cash

**2.4 Technology Asset Intensity**

- **Formula:** Technology Assets ÷ Total Assets
- **Trend:** Should increase as business becomes more digital
- **Quality measure:** Revenue per pound of technology assets should improve annually

**3 - THE DASHBOARD VIEW**

**3.1 Green Lights (Transformation Success):**

1. ✅ Cost-to-income ratio decreasing 2-5% annually
2. ✅ Asset turnover improving consistently
3. ✅ Free cash flow growing faster than revenue
4. ✅ Technology ROI >25% and accelerating
5. ✅ Digital revenue >30% of total and growing

**3.2 Red Flags (Technology Theatre):**

1. ❌ Technology spending increasing but ratios stagnant
2. ❌ Asset base growing proportionally with revenue (not achieving scale)
3. ❌ Free cash flow declining despite "efficiency" investments
4. ❌ Technology ROI &lt;15% after 3+ years
5. ❌ Digital channels cannibalising without improving margins

**4 - THE ONE-PAGE EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

Track these monthly:

- **Efficiency:** Cost-to-Income Ratio trend
- **Productivity:** Revenue per Employee Growth
- **Scale:** Asset Turnover Improvement
- **Returns:** Free Cash Flow Margin Expansion
- **Innovation:** Technology ROI Acceleration

**5 - THE ULTIMATE TEST**

You should continue to ask yourself:

>

*"If we removed all technology investments from the past 5 years, would we generate more revenue, have better margins, and stronger cash flows today?"*

If the answer isn't a resounding "No, we'd be dramatically worse off," then your technology strategy needs urgent attention.
